    About Me

    Hello! I'm a soon-to-be graduate from Tec de Monterrey, Campus Monterrey, Mexico, with a bachelor's degree in Robotics and Digital Systems. I'm passionate about robotics and AI.

    My passion lies in developing deployable frameworks that can leverage domain specific data for reliability. While I’m more inclined to Human-Robot Interaction, Robotic Learning, and Mobile Robotics, I ultimately aim to create intelligent systems that bridge the gap between research and practical, real-world solutions.

    My previous projects have required a combination of multidisciplinary skills, such as electronics, mechanics, and machine learning. This convergence of diverse areas is how I like to define robotics—a field where interdisciplinary expertise comes together to solve complex problems. I invite you to explore my portfolio, where these skills and their applications are showcased in detail.

    During my studies, I’ve had multiple first-hand research experience. I was part of VantTec, a student group at Tec de Monterrey focused on the research and development of autonomous vehicles, where I led the localization efforts for an Unmanned Underwater Vehicle (UUV). As part of my studies, I participated in an exchange program at McGill University, where I contributed to developing an innovative approach to acoustic sensing using accessible, off-the-shelf products. Last summer, I participated in Carnegie Mellon University’s Robotics Institute Summer Scholars (RISS) program, where I worked in social robot navigation in a shared airspace.

    Portfolio

    SafeAir: Safety-informed Prediction and Navigation under Distribution Shifts in General Aviation

    A framework that seeks to improve navigation performance in unseen, safety-criticial scenarios by leveraging existing datasets and trajectory prediction algorithms.

    • Adapted an existing transformer model (TF) to extract aviation-specific features
    • Scored dataset scenarios based on safety-relevant metrics and created a distribution shift, with the highest scoring being the most safety-criticial and thus out of distribution (OOD).
    • Enhanced model robustness by incorporating scenario safety score features and integrating collision avoidance remediation into the loss function.
    • Improved collision avoidance by 70% when tested on out of distribution scenarios.
    • Integrated the transformer model into a Monte Carlo Tree Search for socially-aware, long-horizon trajectory planning.
